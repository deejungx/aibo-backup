# Sahayata Sprint Ritual - Launch Playbook
## "Here's what we're doing starting Monday"

---

## 🗓️ YOUR FIRST SPRINT (Starting [DATE])

### Before Monday: Prep (1 hour)

**Dipesh + Arun:**
1. List top 3 customer signals from recent discovery (write them down)
2. Pick 6-8 backlog items for sprint 1
3. Create shared sprint board (label each item Red/Yellow/Green by signal priority)
4. Pick sprint goal: "This week we're solving [customer need] by shipping [X, Y, Z]"

**Comms:**
- Announce to team: "We're trying a lean sprint ritual. Here's the calendar. First planning Monday 9 AM."
- Send this doc + the ritual design

---

## 📋 TEMPLATES FOR EACH RITUAL

### **Monday Planning (30 min) - TEMPLATE**

**Pre-meeting (shared doc):**
```
## Sprint [X] Planning - Monday [DATE]

### Last Sprint Retro Insights
- [1-2 bullet points from Friday retro]

### Customer Signals This Week (via Arun)
1. Signal #1: [What customers said]
   → Why it matters: [impact]
2. Signal #2: ...
3. Signal #3: ...

### This Sprint's Goal
We're solving **[customer problem]** by shipping:
- Backlog item 1 (Red priority)
- Backlog item 2 (Red priority)
- Backlog item 3 (Yellow priority)

### Backlog (Pre-Prioritized)
| Item | Signal | Owner | Status |
|------|--------|-------|--------|
| RAG pipeline fix | Signal #1 | [TBD] | New |
| Modular prompts | Signal #1 | [TBD] | New |
...

---
**Meeting agenda (live, 30 min):**
1. Recap retro insights (2 min) - "What are we learning?"
2. Arun presents signals (5 min) - "What did customers say?"
3. Team confirms sprint goal (3 min) - "Are we solving the right thing?"
4. Finalize assignments (10 min) - "Who's leading what?"
5. Check-in: Any blockers before we start? (5 min)
6. Done. Everyone has clarity.
```

---

### **Tue-Fri Daily Post (async, Slack/Discord) - TEMPLATE**

**Post in #sahayata-daily thread:**
```
**[Name] - [Day]**
Yesterday: [1-2 lines of what you shipped/worked on]
Blocker: [If stuck, name it. If not, skip]
Today: [What you're doing today]

Example:
**Sarah - Tuesday**
Yesterday: Finished RAG chunking logic, basic tests passing
Blocker: Need clarity on how prompts interact with different model temps
Today: Pair on prompt modularization with Jason
```

**Culture notes:**
- Keep it short. 1-2 lines each section.
- Read the thread. React with 👍 if someone solved your blocker.
- Don't reply unless they asked or you have critical info.
- Consistency matters more than perfection (most people post every day, some skip—that's fine).

---

### **Thursday Huddle (30 min) - TEMPLATE**

**Meeting agenda:**

```
**Thursday Sync - [DATE]**

**1. Demo/Show & Tell (10 min)**
- "Here's what we shipped this week"
- Screen share or quick walkthrough
- Goal: Everyone stays connected to what's moving

**2. Blockers & Problem Solving (15 min)**
- Pull from daily posts + new things
- Each blocker: Owner names it, team helps unblock
- Write down decisions made here

**3. Morale & Wins (5 min)**
- "How are we feeling?"
- Celebrate something that went well
- Flag if anyone's underwater

**Result:** Everyone knows what shipped, blockers are owned, team is connected.
```

---

### **Friday Retro (30 min) - TEMPLATE**

**Pre-meeting (shared doc, optional):**
```
## Friday Retro - [DATE]

What went well this week?
- [People add 1-2 bullets]

What was hard?
- [People add blockers/struggles]

Who helped you this week?
- [People note names for kudos round]
```

**Meeting agenda (live, 30 min):**

```
**1. What Went Well (5 min)**
- Read the doc bullets
- Emphasize: Celebrate the wins

**2. What Was Hard (5 min)**
- Read blockers
- Tone: Blameless. "How can we improve?" not "Who messed up?"
- Pick ONE thing to try differently next sprint

**3. Kudos Round (10 min)**
- Go-around: Each person names someone who helped them
- Be specific: "What did they do? Why did it matter?"
- Write it down in a doc (keep the memory)

**4. One Small Improvement (5 min)**
- Team picks ONE thing to try next sprint
- Not a big overhaul. Small experiment.
- Example: "Let's try doing daily posts async instead of sync"

**Vibe:** Celebrate, learn, appreciate. End on a high note.
```

### **Monthly Recap (60 min) - 1st Friday of Month - TEMPLATE**

**Prep (shared doc, due day-of):**
```
## Monthly Recap - [MONTH]

### What We Shipped
- Backlog item 1: [brief desc, impact]
- Backlog item 2: ...

### Customer Impact (Arun fills this)
- What we learned:
- What changed for customers:
- Top ask for next month:

### Kudos Nominations
[People pre-write kudos they want to give]
- "[Name] because [specific thing, why it mattered]"
- "[Name] because ..."
```

**Meeting agenda (live, 60 min):**

```
**1. What We Shipped (10 min)**
- Walk through backlog, customer impact
- Screenshot/demo key wins
- "Here's what we did together"

**2. Customer Impact Conversation (15 min)**
- Arun presents: signals, what changed, learning
- Team responds: "How does this change what we focus on?"
- Align on top 3 things for next month

**3. Kudos Ceremony (15 min)**
- Facilitator reads each kudos aloud
- People applaud/react (live or emoji in Slack)
- Write it down in a permanent doc (team memory)
- Keep it ceremonial. This matters.

**4. Month Ahead (15 min)**
- Top 3 focuses for next month
- Themes based on customer signals + team goals
- "Next month, we're focused on: [1], [2], [3]"

**5. Anything We Should Stop? (5 min)**
- Quick check: Any ritual feeling clunky?
- Any team process that's not working?
- Make one small tweak if needed

**Result:** Everyone knows what happened, feels appreciated, aligned on what's next.
```

## 🚀 FIRST SPRINT SEQUENCE

**Friday (end of today):** Share this playbook. Let team read it.

**Monday 9 AM:** Sprint planning
- 5 min: "This is why we're trying this"
- 25 min: Run the planning agenda above
- Everyone leaves with: sprint goal + their assignment

**Tue-Fri:** Daily async posts start

**Thursday 9 AM:** First huddle
- Keep it short. Demo what's real.
- Ask for blockers.
- If nothing's blocking, it's fine. Move on.

**Friday 9 AM:** First retro
- Ask: "What worked? What was clunky?"
- Keep kudos genuine (don't force it if vibe is off)
- Pick ONE thing to try differently

**Next Monday:** Sprint 2 planning
- Use retro feedback to adjust rituals
- Run planning normally

---

## ⚠️ THINGS THAT WILL PROBABLY HAPPEN

**"Retros feel fake"**
- Give it 3 sprints. Kudos feel weird at first, then they don't.
- Keep them specific and genuine.

**"We miss the async daily posts"**
- That's OK. Make it optional.
- Some people will do them daily, some just read. Both are fine.

**"9 AM doesn't work for [timezone]"**
- Record all syncs. Post transcript in Slack.
- One person from each time zone tries to be live if possible.
- Async daily posts don't require attendance.

**"We don't have enough backlog"**
- Start with 6-8 items. Add more as you go.
- Arun + 1 person can refine 3-4 items async mid-sprint if you're running low.

**"Syncs still feel long"**
- Cut ruthlessly. Timer exists for a reason.
- If something takes longer than budgeted, move it to Thursday huddle or async.

## 📊 SUCCESS METRICS (After 4 sprints)

Ask the team:
- Are we clearer on *why* we're building things? (tie to customer signals)
- Do we ship faster? (visible sprint goals each week)
- Do people feel appreciated? (kudos happening, remembered)
- Do the two teams feel like one team? (shared rituals, cross-org kudos)
- Are meetings painful or useful? (team should say "mostly useful")

If the answer is yes to 4/5: You've got something that sticks.

---

## 🎯 REMEMBER

- **Lean structure, not bureaucracy.** 90 min/week total. If it grows, something's wrong.
- **Customer-driven, not gut-feel.** Every sprint goal connects to Arun's signals.
- **Appreciation is real.** Kudos matter. Write them down.
- **One team, bridged orgs.** Rituals unite, not divide.
- **Iterate fast.** Adjust after every retro. The rituals will evolve.

You've got this. Start Monday.
