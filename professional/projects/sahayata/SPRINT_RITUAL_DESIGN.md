# Sahayata Sprint Ritual Design
## For 7-person cross-org team (Symbiosis + Telerivet)

---

## 🎯 Core Philosophy
- **Structure without bureaucracy**: Rituals that enable, not constrain
- **Customer-driven**: Prioritization flows from Arun's discovery, not gut feel
- **Distributed-first**: Async where possible, synchronous only when it matters
- **Lean**: 4 structured weekly touches + 1 monthly
- **Cross-org bridge**: Shared rituals that unite, not divide

---

## 📅 THE RITUAL CALENDAR

### **Weekly Rhythm** (4 touches, ~90 min total)

#### 1️⃣ **Monday 9 AM (30 min) - Sprint Planning** ← Kickoff
**Who:** Whole team sync
**What:** 
- 5 min: Last sprint retro insights (standing culture)
- 10 min: Arun's top 3 customer discovery signals (what we learned this week)
- 10 min: Prioritized backlog for this sprint (what gets picked, why)
- 5 min: Owner assignment (who leads what)

**Format:** Shared doc pre-filled with backlog + Arun's input. Meeting is decision, not discovery.

**Output:** Sprint board ready, everyone knows what they're building and why.

---

#### 2️⃣ **Tuesday-Friday (async, 5 min daily) - Daily Sync Post**
**What:** Slack/Discord thread (or async Loom if async-hostile timezone)
- What you did yesterday (1-2 lines)
- Blocker or question (if any)
- What you're doing today

**Why async:** Distributed team + doesn't need live discussion most days
**Culture:** Read, give thumbs-up, jump in if someone's stuck. No pressure to reply immediately.

---

#### 3️⃣ **Thursday 9 AM (30 min) - Mid-week Huddle** ← Course correction
**Who:** Whole team sync
**What:**
- 10 min: Demo/walkthrough of what shipped this week (stay connected)
- 15 min: Blockers + problem-solving (if something broke, fix it here)
- 5 min: Morale check (anything off? Celebrate wins?)

**Why Thursday:** Gives you 3 days to course-correct before end of sprint.

**Output:** Everyone knows what shipped. Blockers are visible and owned.

---

#### 4️⃣ **Friday 9 AM (30 min) - Sprint Retro** ← Learning + Kudos
**Who:** Whole team sync
**What:**
- 5 min: What went well this week (celebrate)
- 5 min: What was hard/broke (blameless; what can we improve?)
- 10 min: **Kudos round** (go-around: who helped you? How?)
- 10 min: One improvement for next sprint (pick ONE thing to try)

**Culture:** This is where appreciation happens weekly. Real, specific, called out live.

**Output:** Weekly memory of what we did well + one small improvement to try.

---

### **Monthly Ritual** (60 min) - 1st Friday
#### 5️⃣ **Monthly Recap + Looking Forward**
**Who:** Whole team sync
**What:**
- 10 min: What we shipped this month (deck/screen share)
- 15 min: Customer impact (Arun: what did we learn? What changed?)
- 15 min: **Monthly Kudos Ceremony** (structured appreciation)
  - Each person names someone who had their back
  - What they did, why it mattered
  - Written + read aloud (feels ceremonial, means something)
- 15 min: Month ahead (top 3 focuses for next month)
- 5 min: Anything we should stop doing?

**Why it works:**
- Connects work to customer discovery (Arun ties it together)
- Appreciation is structured, ceremonial, remembered
- Bridges Symbiosis/Telerivet (shared narrative)
- Looking forward prevents the "we just grind" feeling

---

## 📊 PRIORITIZATION FRAMEWORK
### "Customer Signal → Sprint Goal"

**Input:** Arun's discovery signals (weekly)
- What customers said they need
- What we're hearing repeatedly
- What's breaking their workflow

**Process (Monday planning):**
1. Arun presents top 3 signals
2. Team maps: Which signal matters most for *this* sprint?
3. Label each backlog item:
   - 🔴 **Red**: Direct customer request (highest priority)
   - 🟡 **Yellow**: Supports customer signal (infrastructure, quality)
   - 🟢 **Green**: Nice-to-have (tech debt, cleanup)
4. Pick sprint goal: "This week we're solving [customer signal] by shipping [X, Y, Z]"

**Why it works:**
- No gut feel. Signal-driven.
- Everyone sees the connection: We built *this* because *they* needed *that*.
- Arun owns customer truth; team owns execution.

**Backlog refinement (async, Tuesdays):**
- Arun + 1 rotation (change weekly) refine 5-8 items for *next* sprint
- Not a meeting. Async doc with questions/answers.
- Keeps pipeline ready without extra sync.

## 🤝 CROSS-ORG BRIDGE (Symbiosis ↔ Telerivet)

**Problem:** Two orgs might have different goals/vibe. Rituals must unite, not divide.

**Solution:**

1. **Shared sprint = shared identity**
   - One backlog, one sprint goal, one team
   - Both orgs represented in every ritual
   - (If time zones are hard, async daily posts help; pick one sync that works for most)

2. **Rotation leadership**
   - Alternate who runs retro/planning
   - Someone from Symbiosis leads Monday, Telerivet leads Thursday, etc.
   - Makes people feel ownership, not attendance

3. **Kudos cross-org specifically**
   - Monthly ceremony includes *calling out* cross-org collaboration
   - "Sarah from Telerivet helped us unlock the RAG bottleneck"
   - Builds relationships, not silos

4. **One shared Slack/Discord channel**
   - Daily posts all go here (async hub)
   - Backlog visible to both orgs
   - No "us vs them" conversations

## 📝 SPRINT STRUCTURE (2-week sprints recommended for 7-person team)

### Sprint = 2 weeks, 1 sprint goal

**Monday (Day 1):** Planning
- Backlog prioritized
- Goal set
- Assignments clear

**Tue-Wed (Days 2-3):** Build momentum
- Daily async posts
- Thursday huddle for mid-sprint check-in

**Thu-Fri (Days 4-5):** Deliver + reflect
- Demo what shipped
- Retro + kudos
- Celebrate, learn

**Following Mon:** Repeat

(If 1-week sprints feel right, same structure, just tighter.)

---

## 🎁 MAKING APPRECIATION REAL

**Weekly (Friday retro):**
- Kudos round: Go-around, specific, called out live
- Quick hit, feels authentic

**Monthly ceremony (1st Friday):**
- Structured time, everyone prepares
- Named, read aloud, written down
- Creates memory (shared doc for year-end or morale moments)

**Keys to making it work:**
- It's not optional. It's built in.
- Specific > generic ("You helped me debug the RAG issue" > "You're great")
- Cross-org matters: Make sure appreciation bridges teams
- Save the written kudos. Review them at team reflections.

---

## 🛠️ BACKLOG + REFINEMENT PROCESS

**No separate grooming meeting.** Instead:

1. **Async refinement (Tuesdays, 2-3 hours)**
   - Arun + 1 rotating team member
   - Shared doc: Backlog items for *next* sprint
   - Write: What it is, why it matters, any unknowns
   - Q&A in comments
   - No meeting needed

2. **Planning meeting uses pre-refined backlog**
   - Items are already understood
   - Planning = prioritize + assign, not discover
   - Takes 10 min instead of 30

3. **During sprint**
   - If blocker surfaces, it's discussed in Thursday huddle
   - If new customer signal arrives, Arun flags it for next sprint

   - Did kudos feel real?
   - Do we want to tweak ceremony structure?

---

## 📌 WHAT NOT TO DO

❌ Don't have separate planning + backlog refinement + standup meetings. It's death by a thousand syncs.

❌ Don't let async daily posts become a compliance checkbox. Make them genuinely optional (most people do them, some just listen).

❌ Don't wait until retro to talk about blockers. Thursday huddle exists for mid-sprint course correction.

❌ Don't do kudos in a way that feels forced. Keep it genuine, specific, celebratory.

❌ Don't separate the two orgs into different standups/sprints. You'll permanently silo them.

---

## 🚀 WHY THIS STICKS FOR 7-PERSON DISTRIBUTED TEAMS

1. **Minimal ceremony (90 min/week)**: People won't resent it
2. **Customer-driven**: Everyone knows *why* they're building
3. **Async-first**: Works across time zones
4. **Appreciation built-in**: People feel seen weekly, celebrated monthly
5. **One team, not two**: Rituals unite Symbiosis + Telerivet
6. **Blameless culture**: Retros are about learning, not blame
7. **Visible progress**: Demo every week, recap every month
8. **Flexibility**: One improvement per sprint means you'll evolve the rituals as you learn

## 📝 Next: Test & Iterate

**Your move:**
1. Pick one sprint to test this structure (don't overthink it)
2. Run the 4 weekly rituals as designed
3. In the first monthly recap, ask: "Did this feel good? What should we change?"
4. Adjust for sprint 2

The rituals will evolve. That's fine. The goal is: structured enough to ship faster, lean enough to not feel like overhead.
