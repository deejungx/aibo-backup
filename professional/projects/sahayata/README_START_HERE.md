## 🎁 What You're Giving Your Team

### **The Core Promise**
"We're working more intentionally. Every sprint tied to what customers need. Every person appreciated. One team, not two. And we'll keep it lean (90 min/week)."

### **What They Get**
1. **Clarity** — Sprint goal connected to customer signals (why we're building)
2. **Rhythm** — Same syncs every week (predictable, not chaotic)
3. **Appreciation** — Kudos every Friday + ceremony every month (people feel seen)
4. **Connection** — Cross-org collaboration through shared rituals
5. **Autonomy** — Daily posts are async (async-friendly for distributed team)

### **What They Lose (The Trade)**
- 90 min/week in syncs (Monday planning, Thursday huddle, Friday retro)
- One daily post (5 min, async)
- Some spontaneity (sprints are planned, not ad-hoc)

**Net:** They work more focused, ship faster, feel appreciated. Reasonable trade.

## 💡 Key Design Decisions (Why This Works)

**Why Monday planning + Thursday huddle + Friday retro?**
- Monday: Set direction, everyone knows what they're building
- Thursday: Mid-sprint course correction (if something broke, fix it)
- Friday: Learn + appreciate (close the loop, feel good)

**Why customer signals for prioritization?**
- Removes gut feel: "Customers asked for this, not my opinion"
- Gives team context: They know *why*, not just *what*
- Prevents bikeshedding: Customer need is the tiebreaker

**Why async daily posts?**
- Keeps team connected without live standup fatigue
- Works across time zones
- Optional (some people post, some listen, both are fine)

**Why monthly kudos ceremony?**
- Weekly kudos (Friday retro) keep it alive
- Monthly ceremony makes it feel special & remembered
- Written down = team memory (looks back at year-end, feels good)

**Why one improvement per sprint?**
- Prevents ritual bloat (you don't add everything at once)
- Forces intentionality (what's the one thing that matters?)
- Lets rituals evolve based on feedback

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Won't this feel bureaucratic?**
A: No. 90 min/week is lean. Most teams do 2-3x that in meetings. The key: rituals are for alignment + appreciation, not status reports.

**Q: What if the time doesn't work for our time zones?**
A: Record all syncs. Async daily posts don't require attendance. One person from each timezone can try to be live for planning + monthly recap.

**Q: What if customer signals are weak?**
A: Then Arun needs to spend more time with customers. For now: "Here are the top 3 customer issues *we know about*." Works as a start.

**Q: How long until this feels normal?**
A: 2-3 sprints. First sprint feels intentional. By sprint 3, it's just how you work.

**Q: What if people hate the kudos round?**
A: Make it optional first 2 sprints. By sprint 3, people usually buy in. Keep it specific + genuine.

**Q: Should we track velocity/metrics?**
A: Not week 1. Get the rhythm first. Month 2, if team wants metrics, add them. Keep it simple: "Did we ship faster? Do we feel more connected?"
