# How to Present Arun's Role to Education Consultancies

## The Pitch (30-second version)

"We're building AI agents that handle your student inquiries automatically. But for them to actually *work*, we need to understand exactly how your team works right now — what you do, what tools you use, what you know that nobody else does.

Arun is going to work with you weekly to document that. Not to evaluate you — to *automate* the repetitive stuff so your team can focus on the human side: building relationships, handling tricky cases, celebrating student wins.

We're basically learning your playbook so we can give you a clone of it."

---

## The Longer Pitch (5 minutes)

### Problem You Have (That They Recognize Immediately)

"Your team spends a huge amount of time answering the same questions over and over:

- 'Do I qualify for this program?'
- 'What documents do I need?'
- 'When are the deadlines?'
- 'Why was I rejected?'

Each answer takes 10-15 minutes. You've got 50+ inquiries a month. That's easily **8-10 hours a week** of your team's time on repetitive work.

You can't hire another person just for this. You can't automate it without understanding your process first."

### Solution (What Arun Does)

"Here's what we're proposing:

**Arun works with your team for 30 min/week** to understand:

1. **How you currently answer questions** — We sit in on calls, review past emails, watch your process
2. **What tools you use** — Your CRM, spreadsheets, document templates, how you organize info
3. **What your students ask most** — The FAQ that your team knows by heart but nobody's written down
4. **What only your team knows** — The institutional knowledge (e.g., 'this university values work experience', 'visa processing is 8 weeks now')
5. **Where it's slow** — Which parts of your workflow waste time

**Then we build an AI agent that:**
- Automatically qualifies students (90% of cases)
- Sends the right documents checklist
- Explains deadlines and timelines
- Handles 30-40% of inquiries without your team touching them

Your team goes from 10 hours/week of repetitive answering → 5-6 hours/week of high-value work (mentoring, complex cases, relationship building)."

### What You Get

"By end of month:
- **Less busywork:** Your team stops answering 'Do I qualify?' for the 50th time
- **Better experience for students:** Instant answers, not 2-day turnaround
- **Faster decisions:** Agent keeps track of applications, reminds about deadlines
- **Stronger relationships:** Your team has time to coach students, not just process them
- **Data you didn't have before:** We'll show you where students are getting stuck (so you can improve your process)

Essentially: your team becomes a 'student success coach' instead of 'inquiry processor.'"

### What We Need From You

"Only one thing: **be honest about how you work.**

Arun will ask you:
- 'Walk me through a typical student inquiry, start to finish'
- 'What information do you need to make a decision?'
- 'What frustrates you most about this process?'
- 'What do only *you* know that a new hire wouldn't?'

We're not trying to replace you. We're trying to automate the parts that don't need your expertise so you can do the parts that do."

---

## How to Position Arun (Frame the Conversation)

### What NOT to Say

❌ "We're sending someone to study how you work so we can build a robot to replace you"
❌ "We need your knowledge so we can package it"
❌ "We need all your best practices documented"
❌ "This is about process improvement"

### What TO Say

✅ "Arun is going to be your co-pilot for a month. He's learning how you work so we can automate the stuff that's slowing you down."

✅ "Think of this as: we're reverse-engineering your expertise so we can give it back to you in bot form."

✅ "Your team is amazing at qualification. We want to build a version that runs 24/7 so your team can focus on the students who need real human help."

✅ "We need to understand your world so our agent doesn't feel like a generic chatbot. It should feel like it actually knows your process."

---

## The Meeting Structure (How to Introduce Arun)

### Meeting: 20 minutes with consultancy team lead + 1-2 staff

**Your opening (3 min):**

"Hey [consultancy lead]. We're excited to partner with you on this AI agent.

Here's the reality: AI agents are only as good as the knowledge they have. We can train it on generic education data, but it won't know *your* process, *your* requirements, *your* values.

So we're sending Arun — he's going to spend 30 min with your team each week for the next 4 weeks. He's going to learn:
- How you currently handle inquiries (start to finish)
- What you look for when qualifying students
- What frustrates your team most
- What only your experienced staff know

Then we'll build an agent that knows all of that. It'll handle the repetitive stuff so your team can focus on mentoring and tricky cases.

Arun, you want to introduce yourself?"

**Arun's intro (2 min):**

"I'm Arun. I work with education consultancies to understand their process. I'm not here to evaluate anything — I'm here to learn.

Over the next month, I'll be talking to your team about how you work. I'll ask a lot of questions. Some might seem obvious, but that's the point — I want to understand the full picture.

At the end, you'll have:
- An AI agent that handles initial qualification and FAQ
- Your team freed up to do mentoring and coaching
- Better data on where students are getting stuck

Any questions before we start?"

**Your closing (2 min):**

"We'll start with a 30-min session this week where Arun learns your process. After that, we'll check in monthly to make sure the agent is working for you. If something's not right, we'll adjust it.

You good with that?"

---

## Arun's Weekly Checkpoint (What He'll Say Each Week)

**Week 1:**
"I'm learning how you handle inquiries. Can you walk me through 2-3 recent student cases — from first contact to decision?"

**Week 2:**
"I'm documenting what questions you get most. Can your team compile the top 10 questions they answer? And how long each one takes?"

**Week 3:**
"I want to understand what only your team knows. Can we talk about the unwritten rules? Like, what makes a good fit for your university? What do you know about visa timelines that a new person wouldn't?"

**Week 4:**
"One final check: I want to make sure I haven't missed anything. Are there any parts of your process that feel fragile or hard to explain? Any workflow that feels like 'this is just how we do it but it could be better'?"

---

## FAQ: How to Answer Their Concerns

**"Isn't this a lot of effort on our side?"**

"30 min/week for 4 weeks. That's 2 hours total. Compare that to the 40+ hours/month you'll save once the agent is live. So yeah, tiny upfront investment for huge payoff."

---

**"What if you don't understand our process correctly?"**

"Great point. That's why we check in monthly. If the agent isn't working, we adjust it. This isn't set-it-and-forget-it. We're partners in making it work."

---

**"Will the AI actually get it right?"**

"Honestly? AI is really good at pattern matching. So if you handle 100 inquiries and 80 of them follow the same qualification logic, the AI will nail those 80. The tricky 20 where you need judgment? Those still go to your team. That's the goal — AI handles the predictable stuff, your team handles the exceptions."

---

**"What if we want to change something later?"**

"Tell us. We'll update the agent. This is a living thing. As your process changes, the agent changes. That's the point — it learns from you, not the other way around."

---

**"Are you going to sell our process to competitors?"**

"No. This is specifically for you. Your process is your competitive advantage. We're not packaging it up for anyone else."

---

## One More Thing: Frame It as Collaboration

The key insight: **Arun isn't auditing them. He's amplifying them.**

"Your team is good. Really good. You handle inquiries thoughtfully. You know your students. You make smart decisions.

But you're buried in repetition. There's no magic here — we're just making a bot that does what your team already knows how to do. So your team can do the stuff that requires real human judgment.

That's it."

That framing makes them want to help, not defensive.
