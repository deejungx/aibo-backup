## Arun's Expanded Role: Knowledge Librarian

**New responsibility:** Maintain Knowledge Library for AI agent training (beyond customer signals)

**What Arun documents weekly (1 hour Friday process):**

1. **Sources** — Where customers find info
   - Internal: Knowledge bases, past emails, spreadsheets, team expertise
   - External: Websites, government sites, forums, news
   - Frequency: Daily/weekly/monthly patterns

2. **Tools** — Workflow stack they use daily
   - Communication: Email, WhatsApp, Slack, Phone
   - Data/Documents: Google Sheets, PDFs, CRM, Notion
   - Integrations they wish existed

3. **Intents** — What they're *really* trying to accomplish
   - Examples: Qualification (60%), Timeline Navigation (25%), Logistics (10%), Troubleshooting (5%)
   - Tied to customer outcome, not just keyword

4. **Workflows** — Step-by-step how they handle scenarios
   - Full process from inquiry → decision
   - Which steps are manual (automation opportunity)
   - Time spent on each step

5. **FAQs** — What they get asked repeatedly
   - Top 5 questions, variants, current answer process
   - Time spent per answer → automation ROI
   - Prioritize by frequency

6. **Domain Expertise** — Institutional knowledge in staff heads
   - "Tech background is highly valued", "Processing times are 8 weeks now"
   - Captured through Friday interviews with domain experts
   - Differentiates agent from generic chatbot

**Output:** Fresh knowledge library every Friday → becomes agent design brief

**Agent Brief Example:**
```
INTENTS TO AUTOMATE: Qualification (60%), Timeline (25%)
DATA SOURCES: Past emails (vector DB), requirements spreadsheet
TOOL INTEGRATIONS: WhatsApp, Gmail, Google Sheets
DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE: Tech background valued, 8-week processing time
WORKFLOWS TO SUPPORT: Steps 1-3 (full automation), 4-5 (template-based)
```
